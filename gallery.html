Stories of India

Unity in Diversity

India is home to multiple religions, languages, and traditions — yet people live together in a harmony built over millennia. 22 official languages, 6 major religions, and one shared spirit of Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam — the world is one family.

Ancient Civilisation

India has one of the oldest continuous civilisations in the world — from the Indus Valley cities of 3000 BCE to the living traditions of today, an unbroken thread of culture, knowledge, and human creativity.

Festival Spirit

Every festival in India brings joy, colours, and togetherness among people of all backgrounds. India celebrates over 100 major festivals each year — making it the most festive nation on Earth.

A Cuisine Like No Other

Indian cuisine is a civilisation in itself — 36 distinct regional traditions, each shaped by climate, religion, trade routes, and royal courts. From the delicate saffron of Kashmir to the fiery curries of Chettinad.

Yoga & Inner Science

India gave the world yoga — a 5,000-year-old system of physical, mental, and spiritual wellbeing practised today by over 300 million people across every continent.

Biodiversity Champion

India is one of the world's 17 megadiverse countries — home to Bengal tigers, Indian elephants, Asiatic lions, one-horned rhinoceroses, Ganges dolphins, and over 45,000 plant species.

Did You Know?

  • India has over 1,600 languages and dialects — the most linguistically diverse nation on Earth.
  • Yoga originated in India over 5,000 years ago and is now practised by 300 million people worldwide.
  • India is the world's largest democracy — 1.4 billion people, one vote each.
  • Chess (Chaturanga) was invented in Gupta-era India and spread westward through Persia.
  • The Zero and decimal number system were gifted to the world by Indian mathematicians.
  • India has 40 UNESCO World Heritage Sites — among the highest in the world.
  • The world's first university was established at Takshashila in 700 BCE.
  • India produces the most films in the world — over 2,000 films annually across all industries.
  • Surgery was first practised in India by Sushruta around 600 BCE — 2,600 years ago.
  • India has the world's largest vegetarian population — over 400 million people.
  • The game of Snakes and Ladders originated in ancient India as a moral teaching tool (Moksha Patam).
  • India's space programme successfully reached Mars on its very first attempt — the only country to do so.
